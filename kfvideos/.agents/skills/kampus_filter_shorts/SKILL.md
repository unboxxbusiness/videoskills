## Language: Hinglish & Educator Tone

All scripts, hooks, CTAs, and on-screen text must be written in **Hinglish** (a natural blend of Hindi and English) using a **highly qualified educator tone**. Think of a respected mentor or senior professor talking to a student in an easy-to-understand, encouraging, and authoritative way.

Rules:
- **No Street Slang:** Do not use words like "yaar", "sun", "soch", "bro", "bakwaas", "apne ko", "bhaiya", or street lingo.
- **Educator Tone:** Use polite, direct, and qualified Hindi phrases like "Dekhiye," "Ek baat samajhiye," "Aapko yeh pata hona chahiye," "Aapka decision."
- **Technical Terms:** Always keep academic and career terms in English (e.g. seat allotment, deadline, counseling, placement, AI, scholarship).
- **Roman Script:** Write the Hinglish text using the Roman script (English alphabet).
- **Simple & Clear:** Avoid overly formal or pure (Shuddh) Hindi that students won't understand. Keep it clean and accessible.

Examples of Hinglish Educator Tone:
> "Dekhiye, agar aap IIT placement ke trends ko follow kar rahe hain, toh aapko pata hona chahiye ki is saal averages mein drop dekha gaya hai."
> "Agar aap drop year lene ka soch rahe hain, toh is opportunity ko bilkul miss mat kijiye."
> "Lagbhag 3 lakh students ne iske liye apply kiya hai, par kya aapne register kiya?"

## Shorts Script Framework (Hinglish)

Every generated Short follows this exact structure:

### 0–2 seconds — Scroll-Stopping Hook
Turant curiosity create karo. No greetings. No "Namaste dosto."
> Example: "2 lakh students ne yeh opportunity miss kar di hai — aap yeh galti mat kijiye."

### 2–8 seconds — Kya hua? (What happened?)
Quickly explain the news or situation.

### 8–20 seconds — Kyun important hai? (Why it matters)
Explain the student impact. Professional emotional connection.

### 20–40 seconds — Main Value Delivery
Most useful insight. Use numbers, examples, names of real programs.

### 40–55 seconds — Ab kya karna chahiye? (Action step)
Practical next step. Direct instruction.

### 55–60 seconds — Strong Hinglish CTA
Examples:
- "Deadline miss nahi karni? Is video ko abhi save kijiye."
- "Part 2 ke liye comment kijiye 'YES'."
- "Apne dosto ke saath ise abhi share kijiye."

❌ Never use generic CTAs like "Like aur Subscribe karo." Or informal slang CTAs like "Share kar do."
